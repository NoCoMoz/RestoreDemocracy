Existing accountability measures, like impeachment and ethics investigations, only apply to criminal misconduct or violations of office rules. There is no mechanism to remove legislators who lose public confidence but remain in office due to partisan gridlock, financial backing, or political maneuvering.

How It Works: Article V Convention

The Process

1

Two-thirds (34) of state legislatures must apply for an Article V Convention.

2

The convention is limited to proposing a congressional recall amendment.

3

Once proposed, three-fourths (38) of states must ratify the amendment.

4

The amendment becomes part of the Constitution.

Legal Safeguards

✓

Limited Scope

The resolution explicitly restricts the convention to a single subject: congressional recall elections.

✓

State Ratification Requirement

Any proposed amendment would still need ratification from three-fourths (38) of state legislatures.

✓

Legal Precedent

Supreme Court cases affirm that states retain control over the convention process.

Proposed Congressional Recall Amendment

PROPOSED AMENDMENT – CONGRESSIONAL RECALL

Section 1. The people of each state shall have the power to recall a United States Senator or Representative through a public election.

Section 2. A recall election shall be held if a petition signed by at least twenty-five percent of the number of voters who participated in the most recent election for that office is submitted and verified by the state's election authority. Congress shall have the power to establish uniform verification standards to ensure fair and timely certification of recall petitions, consistent with its authority under Article I, Section 4 of this Constitution. States shall not impose additional requirements that unduly restrict the right to recall.

Section 3. A recall election shall be conducted within ninety days of petition certification. If the recalled member is removed, a successor shall be elected in the same election. If no candidate receives a majority, a runoff election shall be held within thirty days. A recalled official shall be removed from office immediately upon certification of recall election results. A recalled official shall be ineligible to run in the recall election for their own seat and may not run for the same seat in the next regularly scheduled election. A recall election shall only be valid if voter turnout meets at least forty percent of the turnout in the most recent election for that office or matches the turnout percentage of that election, whichever is lower.

Section 4. No recall election shall be held within the first year of a Senator's term or the first six months of a Representative's term. No member shall be subject to more than one recall election per term.

Section 5. If a Senator is recalled, the state may fill the vacancy through a special election or follow its established procedure for Senate vacancies, as permitted under Article I, Section 3 of this Constitution. If a Representative is recalled, the vacancy shall be filled by special election, consistent with Article I, Section 2. If no candidate qualifies for the recall election, a special election shall be held within sixty days to fill the vacancy.

Section 6. Congress shall have the power to enforce this article through appropriate legislation, consistent with its authority under Article I, Section 4 of this Constitution, to regulate the manner of federal elections. If a state fails to hold a recall election within the required timeframe, federal election authorities shall conduct the recall election in that state.

Key Features

  • Requires petitions signed by 25% of voters from the most recent election
  • Elections must be held within 90 days of petition certification
  • Minimum 40% voter turnout requirement
  • Recalled officials cannot run in the recall election or in the next regular election
  • Limits: No recalls in first year for Senators/first six months for Representatives
  • Maximum one recall attempt per term
